Coming down the pipeline

Policy travels. What California and New York pass today tends to show up in Richmond a few sessions later — here's what's spreading, so nothing sneaks up on you.

Already law there Moving / debated Virginia status
Fast-food wage councils
California created a state council that sets fast-food wages — now $20/hour for big chains. New York has debated the same model.
CA law NY debated VA: not yet
The $15+ minimum wage wave
California, New York, New Jersey, and Maryland are already at $15 or higher. Virginia just joined the wave — $15 by January 2028.
CA · NY · NJ · MD VA: law (2028)
Paid family leave programs
Around a dozen states plus D.C. now run state paid family leave programs. Richmond has studied it several times — it keeps coming back.
CA · NY · WA · CO +more VA: keeps returning
Predictive scheduling
Oregon (statewide) and cities like NYC, Seattle, and San Francisco require posting worker schedules up to two weeks ahead. Retail and restaurants: watch this one.
OR · NYC · SEA VA: a 2026 bill tried
AI rules for business
NYC audits AI hiring tools; Colorado's AI law arrives in 2026. Virginia passed its own AI bills this session — this one's already here.
NYC · CO VA: landing now
Consumer data privacy
Virginia was the second state ever to pass a consumer data privacy law — now roughly 20 states have one. Sometimes the pipeline starts here.
~20 states VA: led the way
